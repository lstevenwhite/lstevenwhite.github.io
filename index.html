Research

  • Barren Plateaus in quantuam machine learning directed by Prof. Li-Wei Yu

    • In our research, we mainly focus on the Barren Plateaus phenomenon that may be widely present in different quantum machine learning algorithms in MPS systems, as well as possible methods to solve the Barren Plateaus phenomenon. By calculating the expected values and variances of quantum circuits under different algorithms, we can prove that any quantum machine learning algorithm based on MPS systems cannot be effectively trained.   • Gainless parity-time symmetric optical system directed by Prof. Huanan Li as Tianjin Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program for College Students

    • Non-hermitian systems exhibit many novel physical phenomena quite different from their Hermitian counterparts. Currently, the realization of parity-time symmetry in passive non-Hermitian systems is a frontier in non-Hermitian physics. This project explores the realization of unique scattering phenomena of parity-time symmetric optical systems under evanescent wave excitations in the platforms based on continuous media. We unveil the inherent physical mechanisms by studying the effective Hamiltonian of the systems obtained from the quasi-normal mode method. Our study will promote people's in-depth understanding of non-Hermitian systems.

  • Kitaev spin model directed by Prof. Wang Yang

    • In my current research endeavors, I have embarked on an exploration of the intriguing Kitaev spin model. As a junior majoring in physics, I have recently delved into this fascinating field, seeking to unravel the complexities of its quantum spin liquid ground state and exotic phases of matter. The Kitaev spin model, with its unique blend of anisotropic interactions and the emergence of Majorana fermions, presents an exciting challenge that aligns with my broad interests in physics, mathematics, and complexity. Although I am in the early stages of understanding this intricate model, my enthusiasm and dedication drive me to grasp its intricacies and contribute meaningfully to the field.
